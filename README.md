**Single-nuclei transcriptomic profiling of human myocardium in long chain 3-hydroxyacyl-coenzyme A dehydrogenase deficiency**

Long-chain 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase (LCHAD) deficiency is a rare metabolic disorder that leads to energy production defects and can cause severe cardiomyopathy in pediatric patients. Despite improvements in supportive care, long-term myocardial outcomes remain poor, and the molecular mechanisms driving cardiac dysfunction and recovery in LCHAD remain largely unexplored.

We used single-nucleus RNA sequencing (snRNA-seq) to profile the myocardial transcriptome in a pediatric LCHAD patient and compared it to control and dilated cardiomyopathy myocardium. Our analysis reveals that while partial transcriptional normalization occurs with ventricular recovery, the myocardium retains an ECM-enriched transcriptome suggestive of ongoing low-grade remodeling or residual damage. We also identify a distinct myeloid population likely involved in cellular debris clearance and persistent inflammation, as well as candidate ligand-receptor interactions potentially mediating these responses.

This is the first snRNA-seq analysis of LCHAD-deficient human myocardium, offering new insights into the immune and stromal alterations that may contribute to ongoing myocardial remodeling.
